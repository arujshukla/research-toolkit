\section{Background}

A good paper template should reduce friction rather than create it. That usually means making the common path easy: drafting text, adding references, inserting tables, and extending the appendix as the project grows.

This version keeps a conventional academic article structure while cleaning up typography and visual defaults. The body text remains appropriate for long-form reading, but tables use a more legible sans-serif style to avoid the cramped feel that older serif table fonts sometimes create.
